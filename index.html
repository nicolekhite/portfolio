About Me

Hello! I'm Nicole Hite.

  • Programmer
  • Designer
  • Educator
  • Lifelong Learner

Welcome to my ePortfolio! Please browse through to learn more about me, my passions, and my missions. Growing up, I was always the first in my family to learn about the latest-and-greatest technologies - hardware and software. Although my family could never afford to have high-tech devices, that didn't stop me from finding alternatives or immersing myself in technologies we could afford. When I find something I love, I embrace it and it becomes a motivating factor in my life.

I'm now known as the "certified nerd" of my extended family, as I graduated Summa Cum Laude in Spring 2021 with a Bachelors of Science in Computer Science (CS) with a concentration in Web & Mobile Applications and a minor in Cognitive Science from the University of North Carolina at Charlotte.

During my time in undergrad, I realized that not only did I love learning, but I loved making an impact on how others learn. So far, I've made this impact directly though mentoring and teaching numerous university CS students, and creating a professional development course for teachers to teach AP Computer Science A online. I've also made this impact indirectly by creating an inclusive/supportive environment so students can focus on their academics, and mentoring students through good study habits, organizational skills, and eduational tools.

These factors have motivated me to get where I am today: getting my Master's of Education Learning, Design & Technology at UNC Charlotte with a concentration in Online Learning & Teaching. I hope to continue making an impact on online computer science education in the future.

My Philosophy

More than anything, I love to learn new things. I want to immerse myself in an environment based around learning and do whatever I can to help create a love of learning for others, whether that be through working on a team to improve tools that help with CS Education, or teaching CS online. Pursuing a career anywhere in the realm of CS education seems the most promising path for me to help as many people as possible to experience the same passion and purpose I feel with computing.
As an educator, I aim to effectively infuse technology into my course to promote the learning objectives.
As a programmer and designer, I aim to create accessible, relevant, and user-friendly programs and applications.
As a lifelong learner, I aim to continually grow by actively challenging myself with new skills and new experiences to solve past, present, and future problems.

Learning, Design & Technology
at UNC Charlotte

In today’s rapidly-evolving technological landscape, learning, design and technology professionals, equipped with cutting-edge experience in burgeoning trends perform vital functions in a variety of fields such as corporate enterprises, healthcare, government, engineering, finance, education, and more. UNC Charlotte's Learning, Design and Technology (LDT) program helps students develop deep understanding of learning sciences, instructional design, and educational technology in addition to technology integration, online learning, and multimedia production - preparing students to pursue their career ambitions with confidence in their foundational knowledge, practical skills, and ability to adapt to the fast-paced evolution of technology in every forum and facet of our day-to-day lives.

The M.Ed. in Learning, Design, and Technology program at UNC Charlotte has three concentrations. My selected concentration is online learning and teaching. This concentration includes courses such as "Instructional Multimedia Development," and "Design, Development, and Evaluation of Online Learning Systems" and prepares me to design, develop, and mange online learning and teaching.
